Capital city of Canada

Ottawa is located in the southern portion of the province of Ontario, at the confluence of the Ottawa River and the Rideau River.
Ottawa borders Gatineau, Quebec, and forms the core of the Ottawa–Gatineau census metropolitan area (CMA) and the National Capital Region (NCR)1.

Ottawa is the political centre of Canada and headquarters to the federal government. The city houses numerous foreign embassies, key buildings, organizations,
and institutions of Canada's government, including the Parliament of Canada, the Supreme Court,
the residence of Canada's viceroy, and Office of the Prime Minister.

Note:

1: The National Capital Region, also referred to as Canada's Capital Region and Ottawa–Gatineau, is an official federal designation for the Canadian capital of Ottawa, Ontario, the neighbouring city of Gatineau, Quebec, and surrounding suburban and exurban communities.
